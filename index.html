Design

Browser testing

Browser testing no longer means attempting to make a website look the same in browsers of different capabilities or on devices with different size screens. It does mean ensuring that a person's experience of a design should be appropriate to the capabilities of a browser or device. Therefore, your site will very likely look different on desktop, mobile, and tablet devices, and may look slightly different in different browsers.

Desktop browser testing

test work in current versions of major desktop browsers including Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Internet Explorer. also test to ensure Microsoft Internet Explorer 9 users get an appropriate, usable, but possibly different, experience. won't test in other older browsers unless you specify otherwise. If you need an enhanced design for an older browser, can provide a separate estimate for that.

Mobile browser testing

Testing popular small-screen devices is essential in ensuring that a person's experience of a design is appropriate to the capabilities of the device they're using. test work in:

iOS:Safari and Google Chrome

Android: Google Chrome, Firefox and the stock Android browser

currently don't test Blackberry OS or Blackberry QNX, Opera Mobile, Symbian or other mobile browsers. If you need to test using these, can provide a separate estimate for that.
